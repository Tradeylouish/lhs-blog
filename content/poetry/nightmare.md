The beast extends its talons and its tongue  
For me the bells have surely tolled and rung  
The drool has pooled about my toes and dress  
With it my blood is soon to coalesce  

But though my body screams with primal fear  
Its jaws have not a canine front to rear  
Confusion breaks the hold on life and limb  
Its mouth I pluck a tooth from on a whim  

It steps back shrinking down in size aghast  
Its face a man's that shatters into glass  
The nightmare's end comes with a lucid calm  
I wake in peace though something's clutched in palm
